How Veilr Works
1. Add a contact, once
Each person generates their own password. You enter both — yours and theirs — when adding the contact. Veilr never asks for them again.
2. Type, lock, send
Type your message, tap Lock, then tap Send to jump straight into WhatsApp (or whichever app you chose) with the encrypted text ready to paste.
3. Paste to unlock
Copy the encrypted text you received, open Veilr, paste it into the same box. It decrypts automatically — no extra taps.
Why it can't be broken
Veilr uses AES-256-GCM, the same encryption standard used by banks and governments. Your password and theirs are combined into one key — neither password alone can unlock a message. Every message uses a new random salt, so identical messages never look the same twice. There are more possible keys than atoms in the observable universe.

Terms & Privacy
Purpose
Veilr is built for personal privacy between people who trust each other. It is not intended for illegal activity, harassment, or evading law enforcement. By using Veilr, you agree to comply with the laws of your country.
Privacy Policy
Veilr collects nothing. There is no server, no account, no analytics, no tracking. All passwords and contacts are stored only on your own device, in your browser's local storage. We cannot see your messages, your contacts, or your passwords — even if we wanted to, because we never receive them.
Terms of Use
You are responsible for keeping your own passwords safe. If a password is lost, messages encrypted with it cannot be recovered — this is by design, the same way no company can recover a lost encryption key. Veilr is provided as-is, at your own risk, with no warranty of any kind.
